The Diagnostic

Before you try to scale it, find out what is actually holding it back.

Most founders do not have a revenue problem.
They have a business that still depends too heavily on them,
and revenue is where that problem shows up first.

The Stress Tests are built to tell you one thing fast. Can your business keep producing, growing, and holding together without needing you at the center of everything?

Two versions. Ten questions each. Immediate results.

For Service Based Founders

Service Business Stress Test

Ten questions that show whether your service business is a real business, or a job that still depends on your constant involvement. Measure your dependency across the areas that matter most, including delivery, decision making, client access, team autonomy, and revenue resilience.

The result is immediate. The weak spots get clearer. What you do next is up to you.

Take the Service Business Diagnostic
For Product Based Founders

Product Business Stress Test

This diagnostic is for founders selling physical or digital products who want to know whether the business can really move without them. It reveals where dependency is hiding in inventory, distribution, operations, and overlooked revenue opportunities.

Know the number. See the weakness. Build something stronger.

Take the Product Business Diagnostic
How It Works

Ten minutes to take.
A lot longer to ignore.

This is not fluff. It is not a lead magnet pretending to be insight.
It is a fast, clear read on where the business stands right now.

i.

Answer Ten Questions

Each question targets a critical part of the business, including delivery, decision making, client access, team autonomy, and revenue resilience. Answer honestly. If you want a useful result, do not game it.

ii.

Receive Your Score

Your dependency score shows up immediately. You will see what is strong, what is shaky, and what is putting the rest of the business under pressure.

iii.

Get The Prescription

You do not just get a score. You get the next moves. Clear priorities. Clear pressure points. Clear direction on what needs your attention first.

A Note
