4
How to build and protect
your digital presence
  • Protect consent and usage rights from day one — your voice and likeness are biometric data. The platforms you use to create your clone must have documented terms that protect your ownership, your usage rights, and your ability to remove or revoke access.
  • Be explicit with your audience about avatar use — audiences accept AI avatars when they know what they are. Transparency is not just ethical — it is what maintains the trust that makes your expertise worth cloning in the first place.
  • Start with voice before avatar — a high-quality voice clone of your speaking style is immediately deployable in course narration, audio content, and personalised messages. The visual avatar has higher production complexity — build the use case before the tool.
  • Build the content pipeline that the avatar will deliver — a digital avatar without a content strategy is a novelty. Design the delivery schedule, the formats, and the update cadence before investing in production. The infrastructure justifies the build.
  • Position avatar licensing as an enterprise product — the highest-value avatar application is licensing your expert presence to organisations that want to use your credibility in their client-facing content. Price this at the level of a senior advisory retainer.
