How to land your
first institutional
licensing deal
  • Speak at their events first — a keynote or breakout session at an association conference is the lowest-barrier audition for a licensing conversation. Deliver value publicly before proposing commercially.
  • Target the education director or VP of programmes — not the marketing team. The person with the budget for content is the one responsible for delivering value to members.
  • Propose a pilot cohort first — a single licensed cohort at a reduced fee generates the testimonials, completion data, and outcomes story that justifies the full annual deal
  • Name your curriculum and protect it — a named, documented methodology with a clear outcomes map is what distinguishes a licensable product from a generic workshop
  • Build in annual renewal as standard — licensing agreements should always include annual renewal terms, update rights fees, and exclusivity clauses where appropriate
