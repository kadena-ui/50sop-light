Why one workshop will
never be enough —
and what to offer instead
Every organisation that hires you for a workshop has the same problem after you leave: the insight fades, the behaviour reverts, and the manager who attended goes back to doing exactly what they were doing before.
That is not a content problem. It is a design problem.
The Chief Learning Architect does not deliver a training event. They design the full capability-building system — the learning pathways, the reinforcement structures, the knowledge ecosystem, and the measurement framework that makes change actually stick. That is a retained, strategic role — not a day-rate delivery.

How to make the shift
from trainer to
learning architect
  • Stop quoting for workshops — start proposing learning systems. The next time a client asks for a training day, respond with a 90-day capability-building proposal instead.
  • Diagnose before you design — a learning needs assessment is both the entry-point engagement and the document that justifies everything that follows
  • Name and own your methodology — a Chief Learning Architect with a proprietary learning design framework commands a fundamentally different conversation than a trainer with a slide deck
  • Target the CLO and CPO directly — Chief Learning Officers and Chief People Officers have the budget, the mandate, and the problem. They are your buyer.
  • Build in quarterly reviews as standard — every CLA engagement includes a quarterly review. That review is also a renewal conversation.
