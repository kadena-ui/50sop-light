What a Donor Advised
Fund is — and why
it matters to you
A Donor Advised Fund is a charitable giving account held at a sponsoring organisation — typically a community foundation or financial institution. A donor contributes assets, receives an immediate tax deduction, and then recommends grants to charities over time.
For consultants, the DAF ecosystem matters for two reasons.
First: your nonprofit clients can receive DAF grants — if they are positioned correctly and know how to be found by DAF holders.
Second: you can advise high-net-worth individuals on their DAF strategy — and get paid as their philanthropic advisor.

Donor Advised Fund · How it works
Who contributes
Individuals, families, and businesses with appreciated assets or cash to donate
Tax benefit
Immediate deduction at contribution — grants can be distributed over years
Who holds the fund
Community foundations, Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, and similar
Average account size
$175,000 — with over $230B held nationally

How to position yourself
in the DAF ecosystem
  • Understand the sponsoring organisations — Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, and community foundations each have different grant-making cultures. Know them all.
  • Build relationships with wealth managers — financial advisors manage DAF accounts but rarely advise on grant strategy. You fill the gap they cannot fill.
  • Publish educational content on DAFs — most donors and most nonprofits do not understand how DAFs work. The consultant who explains it becomes the authority.
  • Help nonprofits build a DAF cultivation strategy — identifying likely DAF holders in their donor database and creating targeted cultivation plans
  • Offer a DAF diagnostic as a starting engagement — a short assessment of a nonprofit's DAF readiness creates urgency and a clear path to the full engagement
