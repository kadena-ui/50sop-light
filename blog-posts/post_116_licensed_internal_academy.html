How to build the box
from what you already have
  • Audit your existing content first — every workshop you have delivered, every facilitation guide you have written, and every assessment you have built is raw material. The academy is not built from scratch — it is assembled from what already exists.
  • Use AI to convert delivery content into self-paced format — AI tools can transform workshop facilitation notes into video scripts, convert slide decks into workbook exercises, and generate quiz questions from any existing content in minutes rather than weeks.
  • Build the internal trainer certification before the sale — organisations need to know their people can deliver it. A documented certification process for internal trainers is the feature that converts a proposal into a signed agreement.
  • Name the academy and protect the brand — a named academy with a distinctive identity is an asset. "The Leadership Excellence Academy powered by [Your Methodology]" is something an organisation will promote internally. A generic training pack is not.
  • Require annual content updates in the licence — AI is evolving. Regulations are changing. Best practice is shifting. Build annual content refresh into the licence fee so the academy stays current — and the renewal is justified every year.
