Why dormant clients

asset in any business
Acquiring a new client costs 5 to 25 times more than reactivating an existing one. Most businesses know this — and still spend the majority of their marketing budget on acquisition while their past client database collects dust.
A past client already knows the business. They have already paid once. They left not because of a fundamental problem — usually because life moved on, the timing changed, or nobody followed up. 4
How to sell this to
clients who think
their list is dead
  • Lead with the database audit — before any sequence is built, segment the dormant list by recency, purchase history, and last touchpoint. The audit reveals the revenue opportunity hiding in plain sight — and makes the build feel inevitable.
  • Show the maths before the proposal — if a client has 2,000 dormant contacts and a 5% reactivation rate at a $500 average order value, the programme pays for itself before the third conversion. That calculation closes faster than any proposal.
  • Offer a performance component — a revenue share model aligns your incentive with the client's outcome and removes the upfront budget objection. For clients with strong databases, this is often the fastest path to a signed agreement.
  • Target service businesses with long client histories — dentists, accountants, coaches, agencies, and consultants all have years of past clients who simply drifted away. The longer the history, the larger the opportunity.
  • Position it as revenue recovery, not marketing — "marketing" triggers budget concerns. "Revenue recovery from assets you already own" triggers curiosity. The framing determines whether the conversation continues.
