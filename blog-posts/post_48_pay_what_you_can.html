Why pay-what-you-can
fails — and when
it works
Pay-what-you-can fails when it has no floor, no structure, and no clear signal of what the offer is actually worth.
When it is chaos with good intentions, it attracts the wrong buyers and repels the right ones.
Pay-what-you-can works when it is structured as a tiered access model — with a clear full price, a clearly communicated minimum, and an explicit value signal at every level.
The goal is not to let people choose any number.
It is to let people choose their tier — with dignity and clarity at each one.

3
The rules that protect
the model and your
brand
✓
Always show the full price first — the value must be established before the access tier is introduced
✓
Set a floor and hold it — below your minimum, the offer simply does not exist. No exceptions.
✓
Limit access-tier capacity — scarcity protects the standard and motivates sustainers to fund it
✗
Never apologise for the full price — the access tier exists because of the full price, not in spite of it
✗
Never make it the default offering — PWYW is one offer in your ecosystem, not the identity of your brand

What a well-designed
PWYW offer
builds for you
  • An expanded audience — people who cannot pay full rate today become clients, referrers, and advocates when they can
  • Social proof at scale — a larger community of people experiencing your work generates more testimonials, shares, and referrals
  • A generosity signal that attracts sustainers — high-capacity buyers actively seek out offers that let them fund access for others
  • A warm pipeline — access-tier buyers who experience a transformation become the most motivated buyers of your premium offers later
  • A brand story worth telling — the access programme becomes content, media, and a living demonstration of your values in action
