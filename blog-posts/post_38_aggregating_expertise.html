The curator is not
less valuable than
the expert
Netflix does not make every show. It curates which shows belong in the same room — and charges for access to the collection.
Spotify does not create the music. It curates the playlists — and charges for the experience of not having to find the right song yourself.
Curation is its own form of expertise.
Knowing which experts to trust, which voices belong in the same room, and how to package them into a coherent experience for a specific audience — that is a skill that commands its own premium.

What makes an aggregator
business defensible
  • Your curation standards — not everyone gets in. The exclusivity of your selection process is your primary competitive advantage.
  • Your audience's trust in you — they are not buying the expert. They are buying your judgment about which experts are worth their time.
  • The network effect — each new expert makes the platform more valuable for every existing participant
  • Your editorial point of view — a curated collection without a distinct perspective is just a list. Your POV is the product.
  • Recurring relationships — experts who generate results from your platform renew. The business compounds.
