Why boards are the
highest-ticket audience
in the AI advisory market
Boards of directors have a fiduciary responsibility to oversee material risks to the organisations they govern. In 2026, AI is a material risk. Regulators, investors, and insurers are increasingly asking boards to demonstrate active AI oversight — and most boards have no structured way to do that.
The board member who cannot answer basic questions about their organisation's AI use is exposed. The consultant who can walk an entire board through AI governance — in plain language, in two hours, with practical frameworks — is operating at the highest possible level of strategic access.
Low volume. High ticket. Exceptional positioning. One board briefing leads to the next through the network that only exists at that altitude.

How to get in front
of boards without
a board contact
  • Target governance consultants and board advisors — the professionals who advise boards on governance structure already have board access and will refer AI specialists they trust
  • Write for audit committee chairs and board directors — a bylined article in a director-focused publication positions you as a board-level resource before any meeting is requested
  • Speak at director education programmes — NACD, the Institute of Directors, and governance associations run director education events that attract exactly your ideal audience at scale
  • Offer a 60-minute complimentary briefing to one board — a single well-executed board briefing generates referrals to every other board that director serves on. Directors sit on multiple boards simultaneously.
  • Build a board-specific AI governance framework — a named, documented governance framework positions you as the authority rather than a generalist consultant. Boards hire frameworks, not opinions.
