How to build it without
rebuilding yourself
  • Document what you already do — the questions you answer repeatedly, the frameworks you share instinctively, the advice that transforms. That is your curriculum.
  • Set an intake process — an application or waitlist filters for commitment. People who apply value the access more than people who simply show up.
  • Build asynchronous first — recorded content, written frameworks, and community tools serve mentees between live sessions and reduce your time per person dramatically
  • Price it as a programme, not a favour — a named, structured, outcome-oriented mentorship programme commands programme pricing. Your time has a market rate.
  • Pitch it to employers as a benefit — companies pay for structured mentorship programmes for their employees. Your individual programme is their team's development solution.
