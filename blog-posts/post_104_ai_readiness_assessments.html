Why AI readiness is
the most fundable
assessment in the market
Organisations are under enormous pressure to adopt AI — from boards, investors, competitors, and media. Most respond by purchasing AI tools before assessing whether they have the infrastructure, governance, or talent to deploy them effectively.
The result is predictable: failed implementations, wasted budgets, and frustrated leadership teams who blame the technology rather than the readiness gap that preceded it.
The AI Readiness Assessment is the engagement that happens before the expensive mistake. And because every AI investment conversation now starts at the board level, the consultant who provides the readiness framework owns the strategic entry point to every implementation that follows.

4
How to make the
assessment the door
to everything else
  • Position it as the prerequisite, not the product — frame the assessment as the responsible first step before any AI investment. Organisations that skip readiness assessments waste implementation budgets. That framing makes it essential.
  • Build a proprietary scoring framework — a named, branded readiness model with a documented scoring rubric is a licensable asset. It is also the tool that makes every subsequent engagement — implementation, governance, training — a natural next step.
  • Partner with AI technology vendors — software companies selling AI tools to enterprise clients need a readiness layer their product does not provide. A white-labeled assessment partnership creates a referral engine.
  • Deliver the report as a board-ready document — the assessment output should be written for a board audience, not just an IT team. When the report reaches the board, the consultant who wrote it is invited to present it.
  • Use the gaps as the roadmap — every readiness gap identified in the assessment is a discrete consulting engagement. A score of 2 out of 5 on governance readiness is a governance programme. A 2 on talent is a training contract.
