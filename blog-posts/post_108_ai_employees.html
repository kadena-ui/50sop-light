4
How to position this
without triggering
fear of job loss
  • Frame it as filling gaps, not replacing people — the most compelling use case is always the role the client cannot afford to hire for, or the function that falls through the cracks because everyone is already at capacity
  • Start with one role and prove it — an AI intake coordinator that books 30% more calls in the first month is the most persuasive possible case for the next three roles. Start small, demonstrate fast.
  • Build the SOPs first — a well-designed AI employee requires a documented process. If the client cannot articulate what the role does step by step, the AI workflow will fail. The SOP work often reveals the real value you are delivering.
  • Price against the hiring alternative — a full-time employee in that role costs $50,000 to $80,000 annually. A $15,000 AI employee implementation with a $2,000 monthly retainer is dramatically cheaper and delivers immediately.
  • License the finished role to other consultants — once an AI employee workflow is built and proven, it is a template. Other consultants will pay to deploy your architecture in their own client engagements.
