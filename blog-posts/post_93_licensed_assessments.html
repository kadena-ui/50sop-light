4
What your assessment
needs before it can
be licensed
  • A validated scoring framework — the assessment must produce consistent, interpretable results across different users and contexts. Validity is what makes it licensable rather than merely shareable.
  • A debrief protocol — licensed practitioners need to know how to interpret results, facilitate the debrief conversation, and guide the client from insight to action. Document this completely.
  • A named methodology — the assessment needs a name, a visual identity, and a clear statement of what it measures and why. Named tools are protectable and marketable. Generic surveys are neither.
  • Pilot data from real clients — before licensing, run the assessment with 10 to 20 clients and document the outcomes. The results data is your sales case for every future licensee.
  • A certification requirement — licensed practitioners must be certified before they administer. The certification protects quality, creates an additional revenue stream, and ensures the tool is credited correctly.
