Why AI agents are the
next frontier of
implementation revenue
Most businesses are still at the automation stage — if this happens, then do that. Automations are valuable but brittle. They break when the unexpected occurs, they cannot adapt, and they require constant human maintenance.
AI agents are different. Given a goal, an agent can research, decide, act, check its work, and adjust — without being told how to do each step. An agent can monitor a competitor's pricing and recommend adjustments. It can research a prospect and write a personalised outreach email. It can review a contract and flag the clauses that need attention.
The consultant who designs and deploys AI agents is operating at the frontier — and the market is not yet crowded.

How to enter this
market before it
becomes commoditised
  • Build one agent for your own business first — the most credible AI agent consultant is one who already runs agents inside their own operations. Your own implementation is your most compelling case study.
  • Start with research and intelligence agents — competitive monitoring, prospect research, and market intelligence agents are the lowest-risk entry point. They augment human decision-making rather than replacing human-facing interactions.
  • Position discovery before deployment — most clients do not yet know what an AI agent could do for them specifically. A discovery workshop that maps their highest-value use cases is the entry product that makes deployment inevitable.
  • Build in human oversight by design — the best AI agent implementations include deliberate checkpoints where humans review outputs before action is taken. This is not a limitation — it is the governance feature that makes clients confident enough to deploy.
  • Charge for ongoing optimisation from day one — AI agent performance degrades as the world changes. A monitoring and optimisation retainer is not optional — it is how you protect the investment you just delivered.
