Why private-label is
the most profitable AI
product model available
When you white-label someone else's platform, you are a reseller. When you build a private-label product for another organisation, you are the vendor — and the economics shift dramatically in your favour.
The organisation buying a private-label AI product wants your expertise and your build capability, but they want to present the tool as their own intellectual property to their clients. They will pay a development fee to have it built, an annual licence to keep access, and a maintenance retainer to keep it current.
You build it once. They sell it forever. You collect every year. This is the highest-value software model a consultant with domain expertise and AI implementation skills can offer — because the buyer cannot get it from an off-the-shelf vendor.

4
How to structure the
deal so you never lose
ownership of your IP
  • Never sell the source code outright without an IP buyout premium — the default deal is a licence, not a sale. If they want to own the code entirely and build independently, the price is significantly higher than any annual licence.
  • Retain the right to build similar products — a private-label agreement should grant exclusivity within a defined category, not across your entire practice. You should be able to build a similar product for a non-competing buyer.
  • Require a revenue share clause if they scale significantly — if the product drives substantial revenue for the buyer, your annual licence fee should have an escalator tied to their user growth or revenue threshold.
  • Document the underlying methodology separately — the AI models, prompts, and frameworks that power the software are separate IP from the code itself. Ensure both are clearly documented and owned before any deal is signed.
  • Build in a reversion clause — if the buyer fails to meet minimum usage thresholds or stops paying the licence fee, the right to deploy reverts to you. Dormant private-label agreements should not block your ability to take the product to market yourself.
