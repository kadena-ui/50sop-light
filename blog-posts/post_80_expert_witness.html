What expert witness
work actually is —
and who qualifies
An expert witness is a credentialed professional retained by an attorney, regulatory body, or arbitration panel to provide independent, authoritative analysis on matters within their area of expertise. Their opinion carries weight because of their documented experience — not their legal training.
This is not limited to physicians or engineers. HR professionals, training consultants, organisational psychologists, marketing experts, executive coaches, DEI practitioners, financial consultants, and industry specialists are regularly retained as expert witnesses in employment disputes, compliance matters, regulatory hearings, and professional-practice cases.
If you have deep, documented expertise — you may already qualify.

How to get found
by attorneys who
need you
  • List with expert witness directories — Expert Institute, SEAK, and Experts.com connect attorneys with qualified experts across every specialty. A complete profile is your most important marketing asset in this niche.
  • Build a formal CV, not a bio — expert witness engagements require an academic-style curriculum vitae documenting your education, publications, speaking history, and prior testimony experience
  • Publish in trade publications — attorneys searching for credible experts look for published authority. Articles in professional journals, association publications, and industry press establish your standing.
  • Network with employment and commercial litigators — the attorneys most likely to need your expertise are those handling employment disputes, professional liability, and industry-specific commercial cases
  • Start with a written opinion engagement — a subject matter opinion report at $3K to $5K is the lowest-barrier entry point and the credential that opens doors to larger testimony engagements
