Why companies will pay
annually for access
instead of per project
The project model requires a decision every time. Every engagement goes through procurement, budget approval, and a proposal process that costs the company time and costs you certainty.
A corporate membership changes the structure entirely. One annual decision unlocks ongoing access — monthly sessions, on-demand resources, and executive advisory touchpoints that the company uses throughout the year without re-engaging their procurement cycle each time.
For the company, it is a smarter way to budget for expertise. For you, it is predictable revenue from a client who has already committed to the relationship.

How to convert a
project client into
a member
  • Introduce it at the end of a successful project — the natural moment to propose ongoing access is when a client has just experienced your value and does not want to lose it
  • Show the cost comparison — a company paying $5,000 per engagement four times a year is already spending $20,000. A $15,000 annual membership gives them more access for less spend.
  • Target the L&D or HR budget — corporate memberships live in learning and development or human resources budgets, not in project or consulting line items. Know which budget you are competing for.
  • Bundle the value visibly — list every element of the membership and its standalone price. Seeing $60,000 worth of access priced at $20,000 makes the decision obvious.
  • Start the renewal conversation in month nine — do not wait until the contract expires. Month nine is when you present the impact report and the renewal proposal simultaneously.
