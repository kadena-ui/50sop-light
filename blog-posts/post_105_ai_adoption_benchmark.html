4
How to produce and
fund the first
edition
  • Survey your existing audience first — 50 completed responses from industry practitioners is enough to produce a credible first edition. Your newsletter, LinkedIn following, or client list is the initial survey pool.
  • Sell the sponsorship before you produce the report — approach one technology company or association with the concept before a single survey is sent. A committed sponsor funds the production and validates the concept simultaneously.
  • Build a proprietary scoring methodology — a named AI Adoption Score with documented criteria is a brand asset that competitors cannot replicate without building their own. The score is the moat.
  • Release findings publicly and partially — a free executive summary drives awareness and email capture. The full data requires purchase or subscription. The distinction between free and paid is where the conversion happens.
  • Commit to annual production from day one — a benchmark report that runs once is a document. A benchmark report that runs annually is a publication — and publications have subscribers, sponsors, and compounding authority.
