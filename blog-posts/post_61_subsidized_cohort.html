What a subsidized
cohort actually

A subsidized cohort is a deliberately underpriced or partially funded group programme designed to serve a community that would otherwise lack access — while generating three business outcomes for the consultant who runs it.
It is not giving work away. It is a strategic investment in your methodology, your proof, and your pipeline — made at a fraction of what a traditional marketing campaign would cost.
Participants fund part of the delivery. A sponsor, grant, or your own revenue funds the rest. The outcomes you document become your most compelling case studies. The relationships you build become your warmest future referrals.

3
How to structure it
so it is
financially sustainable
1
Set the full-rate price first
The subsidy is calculated from a real price. A $3,000 programme at $500 communicates value. A "$500 programme" does not.
2
Fund the gap from three sources
Participant fees, sponsor or grant funding, and your own revenue — never from one source alone
3
Cap enrollment to protect quality
8 to 15 participants. Relationships and case studies are worth more than fee revenue from a larger group.
4
Build documented outcomes from day one
Design with measurement built in. What you track, you can publish. What you publish becomes proof.
5
Run it once before promoting widely
A quiet first cohort gives you outcomes, testimonials, and refinements before the public launch

4
Who funds the subsidy
when you do not
  • Foundation grants — capacity building and workforce development are consistently fundable in most programme areas. Write the cohort into a grant proposal.
  • Corporate sponsors — companies looking to demonstrate community investment will often co-sponsor a subsidised cohort serving their target community
  • Government workforce programmes — federal and state workforce development funds can be accessed to subsidise training for under-resourced populations
  • Trade associations — industry associations often have professional development budgets and member engagement goals that a subsidised cohort serves perfectly
  • Your own premium client revenue — a percentage of every premium engagement funds one subsidised seat. Built into your pricing, it becomes a structural part of your business model.
