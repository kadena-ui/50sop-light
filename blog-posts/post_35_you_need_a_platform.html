What a platform is —
and what it
is not
A platform is not a website.
It is not a social media presence.
It is not a course library.
A platform is an infrastructure that creates value for multiple parties simultaneously — buyers, sellers, practitioners, or participants — and charges for access, facilitation, or transactions between them.
When you build a platform, you stop being the person who does the work.
You become the person who owns the system where the work happens.

5 platform types a
consultant can build
◈
Expert directory or marketplace
You curate and connect — practitioners pay to be listed, clients pay to access them
◈
Certification + practitioner network
You train and credential — certified practitioners pay annually to stay in your ecosystem
◈
Curated resource hub
You aggregate tools, templates, and training — members pay monthly for access
◈
Community + event platform
You host the room — members pay to be in it, sponsors pay to reach them
◈
Data or intelligence platform
You collect and synthesize insight — subscribers pay for ongoing access to your analysis

The platform shift
starts with one
small version
You do not need to build Airbnb.
You need to build the smallest viable version of a platform that serves your niche.
A directory of 20 vetted practitioners in your field.
A curated template library with 10 essential resources.
A private community of 50 serious buyers.
The platform is not a scale-up from where you are.
It is a redesign of who you are in your ecosystem —
from participant to infrastructure owner.
