How to build a council
that members never
want to leave
  • Select for peer equivalence, not just title — the most valuable council is one where every member feels the others truly understand their context. Seniority alone is not the criterion.
  • Use the Hot Seat format — each session, one member brings their hardest current challenge. The council responds from experience. This format is irreplaceable and deeply retentive.
  • Enforce strict confidentiality — what is shared in the council stays there. The confidentiality covenant is what allows members to be genuinely honest — and genuine honesty is what makes the format work.
  • Keep groups small — 8 to 12 maximum — intimacy is the product. The moment the group becomes too large for every voice to be heard, the value of the format collapses.
  • Pitch employer sponsorship directly — many companies will pay for a senior leader's council membership as a retention and development benefit. The L&D or HR budget covers it.
