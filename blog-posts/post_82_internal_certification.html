4
How to introduce this
to a client who only
asked for training
  • Propose it as phase two — after a successful training engagement, the natural next conversation is sustainability. "How do you maintain this without me here every quarter?" is the door.
  • Frame it as capability ownership — companies want to own what they pay to learn. Certification gives them that ownership — with your standards still governing the quality.
  • Start with five to ten internal certifiers — a small first cohort of managers or trainers creates proof before the company scales the programme across departments
  • Retain oversight through standards control — your ongoing role is as the certifying authority, not the delivery mechanism. That distinction is what makes it scalable and recurring.
  • Build in annual recertification from day one — standards evolve. Recertification is not bureaucracy. It is the recurring revenue model baked into the original agreement.
