3
How to launch it
from your existing
network this year
1
Define the founder profile you serve
One sector, one stage, one problem. The narrower the focus, the stronger the cohort and the clearer the sponsor conversation.
2
Map your existing network for three things
Potential founders, potential mentors, and potential sponsors — they are almost certainly already in your network
3
Build a 12-week curriculum from your methodology
What do you know that every social enterprise founder in your niche needs? That is your programme.
4
Run a pilot cohort of 5 to 8 founders first
Subsidised or at cost — to build the case studies, testimonials, and methodology proof before the public launch
5
Approach one anchor sponsor before launch
One sponsorship commitment changes the financial model — and gives the programme immediate credibility

What makes an incubator
defensible over time
  • Your alumni network — each cohort adds to a growing community of founders who refer new applicants and advocate for the programme
  • Your documented outcomes — revenue generated, jobs created, funds raised by alumni are your most powerful marketing and sponsor acquisition tool
  • Your proprietary curriculum — a named, documented methodology that cannot be replicated without your permission or licence
  • Your sector relationships — mentors, investors, and funders who return every cohort because they value the access to your curated founder community
  • Your selection standards — who gets in defines the value of being in. Rigorous selection protects the quality of the network for every participant.
