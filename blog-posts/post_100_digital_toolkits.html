How to build one
and get it in front
of procurement
  • Name the problem in the title, not the solution — "The Difficult Conversation Toolkit" sells better than "The Communication Framework Kit." Buyers search for problems, not methodologies.
  • Price it on a purchase order, not a proposal — toolkits should be priced to be approved by a department head without executive sign-off. Under $5,000 for a single licence removes the procurement friction entirely.
  • Build a demo version for procurement preview — a one-module preview that demonstrates format, depth, and quality allows the procurement team to evaluate before committing. It also closes the sale in the same conversation.
  • Get on approved vendor lists — large companies have pre-approved vendor programmes. Being on the list means your toolkit can be purchased without a new vendor approval process — the single biggest friction point removed.
  • Lead with the annual subscription — one quarterly toolkit at $5,000 per year is more valuable than four individual sales at $1,500 each. The subscription model builds the relationship that leads to enterprise licensing.
