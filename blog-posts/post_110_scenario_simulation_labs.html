Leaders who have been through a well-designed simulation make better decisions under pressure. That outcome is worth far more than any strategy document.

How to build and sell
a simulation lab
without a tech team
  • Start with a tabletop format — a well-designed half-day tabletop simulation using AI-generated scenario inputs requires no custom software. The facilitation and the scenario design are the product — not the technology platform.
  • Use AI to generate the scenario content — AI can produce realistic crisis communications, competitor announcements, financial reports, and media headlines that make a simulation feel viscerally real. The content quality is the differentiator.
  • Target boards and senior leadership teams — these audiences have both the budget and the clearest mandate to prepare for disruption. Board members are particularly receptive to simulation exercises framed as governance due diligence.
  • Sell the debrief as a separate deliverable — after every simulation, produce a written analysis of the team's decision patterns, gaps exposed, and recommended preparedness actions. This turns a one-day engagement into a strategic document.
  • Build industry-specific scenario libraries — a library of pre-built scenarios for healthcare, financial services, or professional services dramatically reduces your design time and makes the product easier to license to other consultants.
