Why one focused day
outperforms months

Weekly advisory calls have a fundamental design flaw: the client leaves each call and re-enters a world full of competing priorities, urgent distractions, and the slow erosion of the clarity they just gained.
An immersive intensive removes all of that. The client is fully present. You are fully present. The problem is examined from every angle. The strategy is built in real time. The decisions that would have taken months of back-and-forth get made in a single, focused session.
For the client, this is a decision accelerator. For you, it is a premium day rate — often 10 to 20 times your standard hourly fee — for work that produces your most compelling case studies.

4
How to design and
sell your first intensive
  • Name the problem it solves, not the format — "Strategy Intensive" sells less than "The Offer Clarity Day" or "The Revenue Design Session." Name the outcome the client wants to leave with.
  • Create a structured pre-work intake — clients who complete a thorough pre-work document arrive more prepared and leave with better results. The intake itself signals premium positioning.
  • Always deliver a written output — the documented deliverable is what separates a premium intensive from an expensive conversation. The document is proof of value.
  • Price it as a day rate, not a project — a $15,000 day rate feels more premium than a "$15,000 strategy package." The simplicity of one number signals confidence in the value.
  • Offer a follow-up retainer at the close — clients who leave an intensive energised are your warmest possible retainer prospects. The natural next offer is always in the room.
