The reason training
fails — and what
actually fixes it
The training industry has a well-documented problem: retention drops by 50% within an hour of the session ending, and by 90% within a week. The investment is made. The behaviour does not change.
The problem is not the training content. It is the absence of the infrastructure that translates learning into practice.
Performance enablement is that infrastructure. Playbooks that guide decisions. Scorecards that measure application. Reinforcement tools that prompt practice. Coaching checkpoints that close the gap between knowing and doing. These are the systems that turn a training event into a permanent capability shift — and they require a builder.

The 5 components
of a performance
enablement system
Component
What it is
Why it works
Playbooks
Step-by-step guides for the most important decisions and conversations
Removes ambiguity at the moment of execution
Prompts
In-context reminders, questions, and cues built into daily workflows
Keeps learned behaviour visible when pressure is high
Scorecards
Measurable standards that assess application — not just attendance
Creates accountability without micromanagement
Reinforcement
Spaced repetition, micro-learning, and practice cycles
Closes the 90% retention gap through deliberate repetition
Coaching layer
Manager or peer coaching checkpoints built into the 90-day cycle
Human accountability compounds every other component

How to sell this to
clients who only asked
for training
  • Lead with the retention data — when you tell a client that 90% of training content is forgotten within a week, the conversation about why enablement infrastructure is essential writes itself
  • Propose it as an add-on first — a post-training reinforcement system at $8K added to an existing $15K training contract is a far easier sell than a standalone $75K build
  • Measure and report outcomes — a PES that produces measurable behaviour change data is the most powerful renewal argument available. Build measurement in from day one.
  • Target sales leaders first — sales teams have the clearest performance metrics and the largest budgets for tools that move those metrics
  • License the system after the first build — a proprietary PES built for one client can be licensed, adapted, and sold to the next without rebuilding from scratch
