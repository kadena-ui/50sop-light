Why trained AI models
are the new category
of licensable intellectual property
For decades, consultants licensed frameworks, methodologies, and curricula. These assets were valuable because they encoded expertise in a structured, reusable form. A trained AI model is the next evolution of that idea — and it is orders of magnitude more powerful.
A model fine-tuned on a decade of proprietary client data, industry-specific terminology, and a consultant's documented decision-making patterns does not just describe expertise — it instantiates it. An organisation that licenses that model gets access to a quality of AI reasoning that a generic foundation model cannot replicate — because the domain knowledge inside the model took years to accumulate and cannot be reproduced by simply prompting ChatGPT differently.

How to start building
licensable AI IP right now
  • Start with a custom GPT and a system prompt — the most accessible form of licensable AI IP requires no engineering. A meticulously designed system prompt that encodes your methodology is proprietary intellectual property that other organisations will pay to access.
  • Document what makes your AI output different — the licensing value is not the model itself — it is what the model produces that generic AI cannot. Document the specific outputs, the accuracy on domain-specific tasks, and the quality difference that your training creates.
  • Protect the training data and the system prompt — these are the core IP assets. They should be treated as trade secrets, kept out of client-facing systems, and never shared without a formal licence agreement in place.
  • Build the API infrastructure before you pitch — a licensable model needs a delivery mechanism. Even a simple API endpoint that organisations can query is enough to make the licensing conversation real rather than theoretical.
  • Price based on value delivered, not compute cost — what it costs you to run the model is irrelevant to the licence fee. Price based on what the model enables the licensee to do — the revenue it generates, the costs it eliminates, or the decisions it improves.
