How to build and
sell this before
anyone asks for it
  • Sell it before a crisis exists — the value of a war room subscription is that it is never needed. The organisations that recognise this are your best prospects. Position it like cyber insurance: the best time to buy it was before anything happened.
  • Build the crisis playbook as the entry product — a $10,000 AI crisis playbook is both a standalone product and the most compelling argument for the ongoing subscription that maintains and activates it
  • Target Chief Risk Officers and General Counsel — these are the buyers who think in terms of liability exposure and retainer relationships. They already understand the subscription model from legal and PR counsel.
  • Run tabletop simulations as lead generation — a facilitated AI crisis simulation is the most visceral possible demonstration of why the retainer exists. Attendees who live through a simulated crisis close faster than those who only read a proposal.
  • Price the break-glass rate high deliberately — the emergency rate is not the product. It is the argument for the subscription. When organisations see the hourly rate without a retainer, they convert.
