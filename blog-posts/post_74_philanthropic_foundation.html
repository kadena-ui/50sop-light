Why a foundation and
a business are not
opposites
Most consultants think of starting a foundation as something that comes after the business — a retirement project, a legacy gift, a future ambition.
The most strategically positioned consultants understand that a foundation and a business can operate simultaneously — each funding the other, each amplifying the other's credibility, each serving a different audience with the same core mission.
The foundation adds tax efficiency, donor access, grant eligibility, and legacy positioning to a consulting practice that is already generating revenue and impact. It does not replace the business — it multiplies it.

The 4 foundation
structures — and which
fits your situation
Private foundation
Full control — highest complexity
You fund it, govern it, and direct all grantmaking. Best for those with significant assets to contribute and a long-term legacy vision.
Donor Advised Fund
Simplest entry point — fastest to launch
Contribute assets, receive immediate tax deduction, grant over time. No governance burden. Perfect starting structure.
Fiscal sponsorship
No incorporation required
A fiscal sponsor holds your programme under their 501(c)(3) — you focus on the work, they handle the compliance.
Supporting org
Partner to an existing nonprofit
Establish a supporting organisation attached to a public charity — shared infrastructure, separate branding and focus.

How to start without
starting over
  • Start with a DAF, not a private foundation — a Donor Advised Fund delivers most of the tax and grantmaking benefits at a fraction of the legal and administrative complexity
  • Consult an estate attorney and a CPA first — the structure that is right for you depends on your assets, your tax situation, and your long-term intentions. Get the right advice.
  • Name it intentionally — the foundation name is a brand asset that will appear in every grant, every press mention, and every recipient's story for decades
  • Fund it from revenue, not savings — a percentage of every consulting engagement funds the foundation. Built into pricing, it is sustainable and structural.
  • Connect the business and foundation narratives — the most powerful positioning is when both tell the same story — your methodology produces results in both contexts
