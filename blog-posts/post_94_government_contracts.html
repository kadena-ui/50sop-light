Why government contracts
are the most underused
revenue stream in consulting
Government contracts are slow to secure and fast to complain about. Most consultants hear "procurement process" and walk away. The ones who stay discover something remarkable: once you are on a vendor list, contracts renew almost automatically, payment is guaranteed, and the scope of work is enormous.
Government buyers do not ghost you. They do not negotiate endlessly. They do not disappear after the proposal. They have a budget, a mandate, and a process — and the consultant who navigates that process even once is positioned for multi-year work that corporate clients almost never offer.

4
How to position for
government work
without losing your brand
  • Start with your own municipality or county — local government is the lowest-barrier entry point and often bypasses formal procurement for contracts under a threshold. Start in the room you already know.
  • Frame your work in outcomes, not methodology — government buyers evaluate proposals based on measurable deliverables. Translate your methodology into job placements, certification rates, and documented skill gains.
  • Subcontract before you prime — partnering with an established prime contractor as a subcontractor is the fastest way to build your past performance record without navigating full procurement alone
  • Keep your commercial clients — government work is a revenue stream, not a pivot. The consultants who position it as an additional channel — not a replacement — avoid the compliance burden while capturing the consistent income
  • Hire a government contracts specialist — the regulations are real and the consequences of non-compliance are serious. A specialist pays for themselves in the first contract they help you win and protect
