3
How to structure it
in 5 steps
1
Name the fund and define the recipient
Be specific — who exactly does this serve, and why does that community matter to your mission?
2
Fund it from your revenue — not from charity
Allocate a fixed percentage of every premium sale to the scholarship — build it into your pricing
3
Run a public application cycle
Open applications annually or quarterly — the process itself generates visibility and trust
4
Document and share recipient stories
With permission, their transformation is your most powerful proof of methodology
5
Invite sponsors to co-fund it
Aligned brands pay to be associated with your scholarship — turning impact into a partnership asset

4
What makes a scholarship
fund sustainable
  • Built into the pricing, not added on top — a fixed percentage of revenue flows to the fund automatically, removing the decision from every sale
  • A clear selection criterion — not everyone who applies receives it. The process protects the value and the community's trust in it.
  • Recipient accountability — a simple follow-up structure ensures recipients engage with the program and produce documented outcomes
  • A public commitment — announcing the fund publicly creates accountability, generates visibility, and attracts sponsors
  • Annual reporting — a one-page impact report showing how many received access, what changed, and what the fund supported next
