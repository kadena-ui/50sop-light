The mistake that turns
sliding scale into
a race to the bottom
The consultants who try sliding scale and abandon it make the same error: they never establish the full price first.
When buyers do not see the full rate before the scale, they anchor to the lowest number. They do not perceive generosity — they perceive low value.
Sliding scale only works when the full price is visible, named, and treated as the standard — and the lower tiers are positioned as access options for specific circumstances, not as the default offering.

The 3-tier structure
that works
Sustainer
$500
Above full rate — pays it forward
This buyer funds subsidised access for someone else. Recognised as a sustaining member. The highest-trust relationship.
Standard
$350
Full rate — self-sustaining
The published default. Covers full delivery cost. No explanation needed to choose this tier.
Access
$150
Reduced rate — application or honour system
For those with genuine financial constraints. Funded by sustainers. Not the default. Not discussed publicly.

4
The rules that protect
the model and
your brand
  • Always show full price first — the value must be established before any access tier is introduced. No exceptions.
  • Set a floor and hold it — below your minimum, the offer does not exist. Flexibility below the floor collapses the entire model.
  • Limit access-tier capacity — scarcity protects the standard and motivates sustainers to fund the next cycle
  • Never apologise for the full price — the access tier exists because of the full price, not despite it
  • Make it one specific offer only — sliding scale is not your entire business model. It is one access offer inside a full ecosystem.
