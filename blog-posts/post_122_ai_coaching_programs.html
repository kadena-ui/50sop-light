Why AI coaching scales

Human coaching is profoundly valuable. It is also fundamentally limited — by the coach's calendar, their energy, and the price point that makes their time available. These constraints mean that the clients who most need high-quality coaching are often the ones least able to access it.
AI changes the access equation without changing the quality equation — when the AI is trained on the coach's methodology. How to build an AI coaching
programme that feels human
  • Train the AI on your coaching questions, not your answers — the most powerful coaching AI does not dispense advice. It asks the questions your clients need to answer for themselves. Document your best coaching questions first.
  • Design the AI for between-session support — the AI is most valuable in the moments when the coach is not available: the night before a difficult conversation, the morning of a high-stakes decision, the moment when momentum stalls.
  • Build progress tracking that feeds your live sessions — AI that logs client reflections, tracks goal completion, and surfaces patterns gives the coach richer data for every live session than any manual check-in could provide.
  • Be transparent about the AI component — clients who know they are working with an AI built on their coach's methodology and trained on their coach's approach accept it enthusiastically. Transparency builds trust. Concealment destroys it.
  • Price the AI programme below your one-to-one rate — the AI coaching programme is not a replacement for your premium coaching. It is an access tier that serves clients who need you but cannot yet afford one-to-one. The two products coexist and feed each other.
