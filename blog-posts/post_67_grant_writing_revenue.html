Why grant writers are
chronically underpaid —
and how to change it
Most grant writers are paid per proposal — typically $1,500 to $5,000 per submission. The problem: proposal writing is the last step in a successful grant strategy, not the whole thing.
The work that determines whether proposals win happens before a single word is written: funder research, relationship cultivation, programme alignment, budget strategy, and narrative positioning.
The grant writer writes the document. The grant strategist builds the system that funds the organisation. One is paid per deliverable. The other is retained indefinitely.

4
How to reposition from
writer to strategist
  • Stop leading with writing — lead with funding strategy. The writing is a deliverable inside the strategy, not the product itself.
  • Propose a retainer, not a project — a funding strategy requires continuous attention. Monthly retainers reflect the ongoing nature of the relationship with funders.
  • Own the funder relationships — the strategist who knows which programme officer responds to which framing is worth multiples of one who only writes what they are told
  • Track and publish success rates — grant success rates are your most powerful positioning data. Document and share them.
  • Specialise by sector or funder type — the grant strategist who owns a specific funding niche commands premium rates and near-zero competition
