Why what AI says about
you matters more than
what Google says
When a journalist, investor, board member, or potential client wants to know about an executive, they increasingly ask an AI system first. What that AI synthesises — from articles, social media, reviews, public records, and content — becomes the executive's effective reputation in that moment.
Most executives have never audited what AI systems say about them. They have managed their Google results. They have monitored their press coverage. But they have no idea whether ChatGPT describes them as visionary or controversial, whether Perplexity surfaces their thought leadership or their lawsuits, or whether the AI their prospects consult paints a picture that helps or hurts the deal.
This is the audit that answers that question — and the strategy that reshapes the answer.

Who to target and
how to open the
conversation
  • Target executives preparing for high-visibility moments — an IPO, a board appointment, a speaking campaign, a book launch, or a merger are all moments when AI perception suddenly matters enormously
  • Open with a live demonstration — ask the executive what major AI systems say about them in the room. Most have never checked. The moment of discovery is the most powerful possible sales conversation.
  • Partner with executive PR and communications firms — they already serve this audience and will refer AI reputation work they are not equipped to deliver themselves
  • Build a corporate version for CHROs — AI perception of a company's executive team is increasingly a talent and recruitment factor. HR leaders will fund team-wide audits as a retention and attraction tool.
  • Price the monitoring retainer as the default close — the audit is the entry. The quarterly monitoring retainer is the product. AI systems update continuously — a one-time audit has a shelf life of 90 days.
